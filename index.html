As a Visiting Scholar at the McGill Edge Intelligence Lab (MEIL), my primary objective is to develop technologies for the efficient implementation of machine intelligence at the edge to enable and support real-time applications.

My research interests lie at the intersection of machine learning, computer vision, and natural language processing, with a current emphasis on the subject of designing efficient AI for edge devices.

Academic background: I am currently a Visiting Scholar at the McGill Edge Intelligence Lab (MEIL) working with Warren Gross, Jim Clark, and Brett H. Meyer. I joined MEIL as a Research Associate in August 2020. My primary duties includeded: execute on key long-term project deliverables; foster strong working relationships with internal and external stakeholders; supervise students and postdocs. I obtained my Ph.D. from the Computer Vision Lab, in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Laval University, Canada. For my Ph.D. thesis, I worked on predicting human activities with uncertainty from short video snippets. I then spent a semester as a researcher at University of Southern California under the supervision of LP Morency in MultiComp Lab. Our main project involved predicting dyadic human activity from partially observed video data.
Industrial background: I started my professional carrier at Stradigi AI. I then joined Thales Canada Inc. as a Research & Technology Lead in AI. I was primarily involved in planning, conducting and analyzing experiments, either with a definite end use (to develop new products, processes or commercial applications) or to broaden scientific understanding in general. I then joined Unity as a Senior Applied Scientist in November 2021.
